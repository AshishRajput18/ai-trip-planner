# Travel Report for Mumbai
## Introduction
Mumbai, formerly known as Bombay, is the capital city of the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is a bustling metropolis with a rich cultural heritage and a blend of modern and traditional architecture.

## Hotels and Accommodations
Mumbai has a wide range of hotels and accommodations to suit all budgets. Some popular options include:
* The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel
* The Oberoi Hotel
* The Four Seasons Hotel
* The ITC Grand Central Hotel
* The Hyatt Regency Hotel

For budget-friendly options, consider:
* The Hotel Suba Palace
* The Hotel Marine Plaza
* The Hotel Sea Princess

You can book these hotels through online booking platforms like Booking.com, Expedia, or directly through the hotel's website.

## Daily Living Expenses
The cost of living in Mumbai can vary depending on your lifestyle and preferences. Here are some estimated daily expenses:
* Meal: ₹200 - ₹500 (approximately $2.50 - $6.50 USD) per meal
* Transportation: ₹50 - ₹100 (approximately $0.65 - $1.30 USD) per ride
* Accommodation: ₹2,000 - ₹5,000 (approximately $25 - $65 USD) per night
* Attractions: ₹500 - ₹1,000 (approximately $6.50 - $13 USD) per person

## Transportation Information
Mumbai has a well-developed public transportation system, including:
* Mumbai Suburban Railway: a comprehensive rail network that connects the city
* Mumbai Metro: a rapid transit system with multiple lines
* Bus: an extensive network of buses that cover most areas of the city
* Taxi: readily available, but be sure to use a metered taxi or negotiate the fare beforehand
* Auto-rickshaw: a popular mode of transportation for short distances

You can also use ride-hailing services like Ola or Uber.

## Weather Forecast
Mumbai has a tropical wet and dry climate, with high temperatures and humidity throughout the year. In June, the average high temperature is around 32°C (90°F), while the average low temperature is around 26°C (79°F).

## Travel Tips
* Be prepared for crowds and long lines at popular attractions
* Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun
* Try local cuisine, such as vada pav, pav bhaji, and bhelpuri
* Explore the city's many markets, such as the Chor Bazaar and the Colaba Causeway
* Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples or mosques

Remember to check the current weather forecast and travel advisories before your trip. Enjoy your time in Mumbai!
